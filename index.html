Opportunities, Challenges, and Threats in Media and Information

Media creates opportunities for learning and connection but also poses challenges like misinformation and online risks. Responsible use ensures safety and credibility.

Media and Information Literate Individual

Someone who can access, analyze, evaluate, and create media responsibly. This person uses media with understanding, ethics, and awareness of its effects on society.

Text and Information Media

Text media includes newspapers, books, and online articles that inform, educate, and persuade through written words.

Design Principles and Elements

Design uses principles such as balance, contrast, alignment, and emphasis, along with elements like color and shape, to create effective visual messages.

Visual Information and Media

Visual media uses images, videos, and graphics to express messages faster and more memorably than text alone.

Audio Information and Media

Audio media communicates using sound—like radio, podcasts, and sound effects—which influence emotion and imagination.

Manipulative Information and Media

Manipulative media changes or shapes perception through editing or selective presentation, often used in advertising or propaganda. Critical thinking is key to spotting it.

Multimedia Information and Media

Multimedia combines text, visuals, audio, and animation to present information interactively. It enhances communication and engagement in digital environments.

Media and Information Literacy Overview

MIL empowers individuals to think critically, verify facts, and use media ethically. It is essential for informed participation in today’s digital world.
