Media and Information Literacy - Second Quarter

Prepared by Joe

1. Opportunities, Challenges, and Threats in Media and Information

Media and information provide great opportunities for learning, communication, and creativity. However, they also bring challenges like misinformation, online privacy issues, and digital addiction. Being aware of both helps us use media responsibly.

2. Media and Information Literate Individual

A media and information literate person can access, evaluate, and create information effectively. They think critically about messages and make responsible choices when using media platforms.

3. Text and Information Media

Text media includes books, newspapers, magazines, and online articles. It is the foundation of communication and helps in sharing detailed and accurate information.

4. Design Principles and Elements

Good media design uses principles like balance, contrast, emphasis, rhythm, and unity. Elements such as color, shape, texture, and space make visuals more effective and appealing.

5. Visual Information and Media

Visual media includes images, infographics, and videos. These communicate messages quickly and attractively, helping audiences understand information easily.

6. Audio Information and Media

Audio media uses sound, such as podcasts, radio, and voice recordings. It creates emotional connection and is effective for storytelling and education.

7. Manipulative Information and Media

Manipulative media are physical or digital tools that allow users to interact with information, like models, simulations, or virtual reality. They make learning more engaging and hands-on.

8. Multimedia Information and Media

Multimedia combines text, images, audio, and video to deliver information in a richer and more interactive way. Examples include websites, online lessons, and digital presentations.

9. Media and Information Literacy

Media and information literacy (MIL) teaches us how to access, analyze, evaluate, and create media content responsibly. It helps individuals become informed, critical, and ethical users of information.
