1. Opportunities, Challenges, and Threats in Media and Information

Media and information give opportunities to learn, connect, and express ideas. However, they also come with challenges like fake news, online privacy risks, and overexposure to digital content. Being aware of these helps us use media safely and wisely.

2. Media and Information Literate Individual

A media and information literate person can access, evaluate, and create information responsibly. They think critically about content and understand how media influences people and society.

3. Text and Information Media

Text media includes books, newspapers, magazines, and online articles. It delivers detailed information and remains one of the most reliable sources for study and communication.

4. Design Principles and Elements

Effective media design follows principles such as balance, contrast, emphasis, and unity. Elements like color, shape, and texture enhance visual appeal and message clarity.

5. Visual Information and Media

Visual media includes images, infographics, and videos. These help people understand information faster and make learning more engaging.

6. Audio Information and Media

Audio media uses sound to communicate messages. Radio, podcasts, and music are powerful tools for spreading information and emotion.

7. Manipulative Information and Media

Manipulative media are interactive materials or digital tools that allow learners to explore concepts, such as 3D models or simulations, making learning more hands-on.

8. Multimedia Information and Media

Multimedia combines text, images, audio, and video into one presentation. It enhances learning by appealing to multiple senses and keeping audiences interested.

9. Media and Information Literacy

Media and Information Literacy (MIL) empowers individuals to access, evaluate, and create media content ethically. It encourages responsible digital citizenship and critical thinking in the information age.
