Media and Information Literacy - 2nd Quarter

Prepared by Joe

1. Opportunities, Challenges, and Threats in Media and Information

Media and information give people the chance to learn, connect, and share ideas globally. However, they also bring risks like fake news, privacy invasion, and digital addiction. Being aware of these helps individuals use media wisely and safely.

2. Media and Information Literate Individual

A media and information literate person knows how to find, evaluate, and create information responsibly. They understand media messages and think critically about what they consume and share online.

3. Text and Information Media

Text media includes books, newspapers, and online articles. It plays a key role in spreading knowledge and preserving ideas for education and communication.

4. Design Principles and Elements

Design uses elements like color, line, and shape, along with principles such as balance, contrast, and unity. These make media more attractive and effective for communication.

5. Visual Information and Media

Visual media uses images, posters, and infographics to communicate ideas quickly and clearly. It captures attention and supports better understanding of information.

6. Audio Information and Media

Audio media uses sound — like podcasts, songs, or radio — to share messages. It helps deliver emotion, tone, and clarity to information.

7. Manipulative Information and Media

Manipulative media involves tools or materials that users can physically interact with, such as models or simulations. They enhance hands-on learning and creativity.

8. Multimedia Information and Media

Multimedia mixes text, audio, video, and graphics to make learning more engaging and interactive. Examples include websites, online lessons, and presentations.

9. Media and Information Literacy

Media and information literacy empowers people to access, analyze, and create content responsibly. It helps them become critical, ethical, and informed users of media.
